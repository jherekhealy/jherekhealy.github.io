Fourth Edition in Colors

The fourth edition of the book is now available on Amazon (UK, DE, FR) as hardcover, printed in colors.

Some chapters have been split and their order has been updated. In terms of content, vanilla option pricing under the spot piecewise-lognormal model has been reworked and now includes newer approximations. The pricing of American options under negative rates adds the latest developments of Andersen and Lake. The sections on the finite difference method have been significantly updated, especially around grid stretching, payoff smoothing, the solution of the linear complementary problem under negative rates, and explicit super-time-stepping schemes. A variety of arbitrage-free representations for implied volatilities has been added, as well as an in-depth comparison of the various techniques on a set of concrete market data. Finally the chapter on stochastic volatility now includes simulation and calibration of rough volatility models. More minor changes include the addition of the SABR approximation for basket option prices along with new details on the Black-Scholes approximations, a more careful summation for Monte-Carlo, the Jacobian based Vega for stochastic volatility models, fixing typos around the pricing of variance swaps, which also now presents more challenging examples.

Third Edition and Code Repository

A third edition of the book is now available on Amazon (UK, DE, FR) as paperback and Lulu as hardcover.

In this third edition, the physical exercise feature is briefly discussed in the first two chapters. The section on how to imply the Black-Scholes volatility has been updated, with the improvements discussed in my blog. More details around the projected successive over-relaxation method for the pricing of American options have been added, as well as the relatively recent policy iteration method, particularly relevant in the case of negative interest rates, in Chapter 3. A more significant update is the presentation of the Andersen-Lake algorithm instead of the Ju-Zhong formula as fast pricing routine for the case of vanilla American options under the Black-Scholes model with constant parameters. Chapter 4 has been updated around random number generation, antithetic variates, and the vectorization of the Monte-Carlo simulation. Important corrections have been made around the pricing of forward starting options with finite difference methods in Chapter 8, as well as to the pricing of knock-in options.

Self-publishing, Amazon KDP vs Lulu.com

Initially, I published my book through the simplest solution, Amazon CreateSpace, now known as Amazon KDP. The experience was overall great. Amazon KDP allows to print the book in the size of our choice. The standard paper quality is good and the cover looks very much like the digitally uploaded picture.

Some people prefer a hardcover to paperback for durability. Unfortunately, a hardcover publication is not possible with Amazon KDP. After searching on the web and reading various articles about other sites, which permit self-publication of hardcover books, I decided to try Lulu.com, even if I was a bit scared by this very negative review. The other options I looked at were either not appropriate for a math book, more complex to setup, or more expensive.

About the cover: implied volatility fractals!

As you will have probably already noticed, the front and back pictures of my book are fractals. But they are not just any fractal. They are generated by coloring the number of iterations needed to solve the implied volatility of a vanilla option of a given price in the complex plane, using a root finding algorithm like Newton’s method or Householder’s method. Each point corresponds to a complex implied volatility guess and the more iterations needed, the darker is the point.
